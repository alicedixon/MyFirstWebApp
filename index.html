Introduction

Women's domestic cricket in England and Wales has grown significantly in recent years, featuring professional teams and competitive tournaments. The England & Wales Cricket Board have recently commited to a complete restructure of professional women's cricket, resulting in a new 3-tiered system.

Teams

As of the 2025 season, Tier 1 teams are fully professional and consist of:
Durham, Essex, Hampshire, Lancashire, Nottinghamshire, Somerset, Surrey, and Warwickshire.

Tier 2 teams are semi-professional and consist of:
Derbyshire, Glamorgan, Gloucestershire, Kent, Leicestershire, Middlesex, Northamptonshire, Sussex, Worcestershire, and Yorkshire.

Tier 3 teams are amateur and they consist of:
Buckinghamshire, Berkshire, Cheshire, Cornwall, Cumbria, Devon, Dorset, Herefordshire, Hertfordshire, Huntingdonshire, Lincolnshire, Norfolk, Oxfordshire, Shropshire, Staffordshire, Suffolk, and Wiltshire.

Competitions

Teams will compete in three competitions this season:

  • T20 Blast
  • One-Day Cup
  • T20 County Cup
